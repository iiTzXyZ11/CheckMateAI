Original Essay:
One major theme in Romeo and Juliet is the power of fate. Throughout the play, the characters’ lives are controlled by forces beyond their control, which ultimately lead to the tragic ending. For instance, Romeo and Juliet meet by chance at the Capulet party, and despite their families’ feud, they fall in love immediately. Their secret marriage and the series of misunderstandings, such as Romeo not receiving Friar Laurence’s letter, show how fate continually influences their decisions. This theme highlights that no matter how hard they try, the lovers cannot escape their destined outcomes. Shakespeare uses fate to emphasize the tension between free will and destiny, making the story both compelling and tragic.
